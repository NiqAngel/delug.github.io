Unsupervised Image Segmentation with W-Nets

Image segmentation involves pixelwise classification, where in we classify every pixel of an input image into one of the classes of Interest. One very good candidate for segmentation is the encoder-decoder network called U-Net. The basic U-Net is a supervised learning algorithm, i.e. we require a ground truth, such as the segmentation mask. However one issue which often creeps up is the lack of a proper ground truth in many application domain. A number of unique algorithm exists in literature, semi-supervised and un-supervised to handle this case. In this session we will discuss the basics of segmentation with neural nets, starting with a simple U-net, explore what classify as supervised, semi-supervised and un-supervised approach and finally we are going to discuss the high level idea of a very recent publication, the W-Net, which is an unsupervised image segmentation algorithm having high potential for improvements in the near future.
